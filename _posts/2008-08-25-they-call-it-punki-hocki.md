In Brazilian Portuguese, consonant heavy words from English can get an odd array of ‘epenthetic vowels’ thrown in to make them pronounceable.  Epenthetic vowels are additional unwritten vowels.  The usual example is ‘faschi foodi’ for &#8216;fast food.&#8217;  The epenthetic vowels I have to deal with most often are for punk rock.  Here in Brazil, it&#8217;s pronounce it ‘punki hocki.’ (The initial ‘r’ sounding like and ‘h’ is typical pronunciation.)  I fell over laughing when I first heard punk rock referred to this way.  I’m getting used to it though.

Check out my friends’ band practice.  I <3 punki hocki.
