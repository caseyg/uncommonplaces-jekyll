  <span style="font-size:small;font-family:Times;"><a href="http://idaimages.files.wordpress.com/2008/06/08-06-08garbage087.jpg"></a><a href="http://idaimages.files.wordpress.com/2008/06/08-06-08garbage097.jpg"></a><a href="http://idaimages.files.wordpress.com/2008/06/08-06-08garbage133.jpg"></a><a href="http://idaimages.files.wordpress.com/2008/06/08-06-08garbage108.jpg"></a><a href="http://idaimages.files.wordpress.com/2008/06/08-06-08garbage151.jpg"></a><a href="http://idaimages.files.wordpress.com/2008/06/08-06-08garbage117.jpg"></a><a href="http://idaimages.files.wordpress.com/2008/06/08-06-08garbage154.jpg"></a>On Sunday morning, Roshan Rai from the NGO Dajreeling Ladenla Road Prerna, the same NGO I am working with, lead a group of school kids on a tour of waste management in Darjeeling.<span>  </span>The tour, which was part of the World Environment Day events, included a visit to the slaughterhouse and garbage chute.<span>  </span>Darjeeling’s waste management system was designed in the 1930’s and hasn’t been updated since.<span>  </span>The impact on Darjeeling’s inhabitants is sever, especially with the contamination of scarce water supplies.<span>  </span>The goal of the waste management visits is to shock the youth into action around reducing resource intake and implementing daily conservation practices. </span>
